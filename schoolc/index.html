About Us

Prosang (Prodyogiki Sangam) is a technology fest hosted in and by Motilal Nehru National Institute of Technology Allahabad. It is an initiative to bring together a talented and technically skilled group of people to showcase their scientific prowess in the form of actual projects as well as ideas in fun and challenging tech-based events.

The selected participants not only get to visit and experience the atmosphere in a National Institute of Technology, but also get to attend lectures from brilliant technocrats from around the nation, technical workshops, laboratory visits and much more. Moreover, the participants get an opportunity to grow amongst the ideas and creations of fellow participants from 20+ colleges and 150+ schools from 20+ cities!

1. A hands-on workshop on fundamentals of Robotics

Robotics is the art of making robots and machines that combine concepts from multiple engineering disciplines namely Computer Science, Electrical and Electronics, Mechanical,etc. Creating your own robot will help you practically learn how engineering is applied. ...

After qualifying for the on spot rounds, all the students will get a chance to attend a hands-on workshop on Robotics. The workshop is designed in such a way that every student will be able to follow what is taught in the class.

Content of the workshop

  • Introduction to Robotics
  • Introduction to sensors
  • Working of IR (Infra Red) sensors
  • Introduction to motors
  • Line Following Robot Problem
  • Circuit Diagram of the Robot
  • Programming the Arduino
  • HANDS-ON-SESSION
The workshop will be of 5 hour with 2 hour hands on session on kits provided by the institute.

Ask Question?

What are the prerequisites to take part in open school championship?
There is no prerequisite to take part in the open school championship.
My school is not tied up with the Prosang, can I still participate?
Yes, you can participate in Project exhibition and Idea Presentation events.
Who will take care of accommodation if we qualify the first rounds?
There will be free accommodation for teams qualifying from the general science quiz and teams qualifying for project exhibition and idea presentation need to pay a fee of 50/- Rs per team member for their accommodation. Accommodation will be provided in the MNNIT Campus.
Is food also included in the accommodation?
No, the food is not included in the accommodation. But there are food court, canteens, hostel messes where you can pay a nominal amount and get the food.
What kind of accommodation is provided?
Accommodation is provided on a shared basis inside campus hostels or outside hotels. One mattress, and enough space for keeping luggage, and other essential commodities will be provided. Girls and boys will be accommodated separately. Number of guests in a room will be decided by Prosang and will be allotted by Prosang Team.
What is the duration of the accommodation?
You will get accommodation for 3 days from 21st (Friday) noon to 24th(Monday) noon.
Shall I carry my ID card with me?
It is mandatory for all guest participants to carry college IDs. This is for your own convenience. Moreover, you will be asked to produce your college ID cards at the time of allotment of rooms.

PROJECT EXHIBITION

This is the flagship event of Prosang featuring 50+ projects from all the participating colleges and schools. It is a platform to showcase as well as find exquisite ideas in the form of projects. The exhibition will take place in MNNIT itself and will see participation from multiple colleges and schools. The projects will be judged by a panel of veteran technocrats and professors and top three BEST PROJECTS will be crowned. Exciting prizes are in store for the teams that manage to bag the aforementioned title.

The event will consist of two rounds:
1) Abstract Submission: An abstract is an abbreviated version of project final report.
It will consist of the following contents:

     a) Introduction: This is where you describe the purpose for doing your project or invention. This is the place where you show your presentation skills and attract the reader towards the project.
      b) Problem Statement: Identify the problem you solved or the hypothesis you investigated. Show the motive of your project as a problem whose solution you are going to give.
      c) Procedures: What was your approach for investigating the problem?
      d) Results: What answer did you obtain? Be specific and use numbers to describe your results.
